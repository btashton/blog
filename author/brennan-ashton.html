Quick Intro

Fri 13 March 2015

I tinker in many things in the world of engineering, especially in hardware and software. So I expect that this blog will drag me into many different topics. And perhaps encourage me to focus on a few of them a little more. I plan on using this blog a notes and thought dump, and I hope that it will be of use to others trying to muddle through problems.

Here are a few projects that I think are really cool:

  • Ombitron -- I work as the CTO of this very cool company and I love it.
  • Fedora Linux -- I have been a very long time user and former contributor. It might not be the distro for everyone, but as a developer I think it's great, and I have gotten to understand most of its oddities.
  • Fedora Linux -- This is an absolutely awesome RTOS for small embedded systems and takes on the look of a POSIX system. Gregory Nutt has done an amazing job with this, and I enjoy working with it and contributing to it. You should expect to see many articles on it.
  • Python -- This is my go to language for the last years. I use it for everything from little scripts to make my life easier, to big data processing engines.
  • iPython -- I use the notebook feature to write reports where the data actually is part of the report and people can see how I came up with the numbers. I think this is really important if you want the reports to be realivent going forward.
  • Flask -- This web microframework is awesome, and I have used it in many projects.
  • SciPy and NumPy also rock for doing analysis. They have replaced MATLAB for me.
  • InfluxDB -- I am really excited about this database and the guys who write it seem to rock. This is really the first project that I have had to mess with Golang.
  • Docker CoreOS -- I have really started to play with these services for doing microservice deployments, and I really like it. I am sure these will come up as well.
